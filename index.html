8th Grade Math Practice

Problem 1: Simplify using exponent rules:
\( (3^2 \cdot 3^{-5}) \cdot 3^4 \)
Hint: Add exponents when multiplying powers with the same base.
Problem 2: Solve the linear equation for x:
\( 5(2x - 3) + 4 = 7x + 10 \)
Hint: Distribute, combine like terms, then isolate x.
Problem 3: A right triangle has legs 5 and 12. What is the hypotenuse?
Use the Pythagorean theorem: \( c = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2} \).

9th Grade Algebra 1 Practice

Problem 1: Solve the system of equations by elimination:
\( 3x + 2y = 13 \)
\( 6x - 5y = -9 \)
Hint: Multiply first equation by 5 and second by 2 to eliminate y.
Problem 2: Factor the quadratic completely:
\( x^2 - 7x + 12 = 0 \)
Answer: \( (x - 3)(x - 4) = 0 \), so \( x = 3 \) or \( x = 4 \)
Problem 3: Find the slope and y-intercept of the line:
\( y = -4x + 9 \)
Then explain what they mean in context (e.g., rate of change).

Geometry Essentials (8th/9th Grade)

Problem 1: In triangle ABC, ∠A = 55° and ∠B = 65°. Find ∠C.
Problem 2: Find the distance between points (1, 2) and (5, 6) on the coordinate plane.
Use the distance formula: \( d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2} \)
Problem 3: Verify if a triangle with sides 6, 8, 10 is a right triangle using the Pythagorean theorem.
